Eco Pass for Foothill College

Vote Yes for the Eco Pass!

The Eco Pass is a pass for VTA buses and light rail. Students with an Eco Pass can ride VTA transportation in the South Bay for free, all quarter long. A small fee, mandatory for all students, would be added to pay for this. De Anza College has already implemented the Eco Pass for $5 each quarter.

Many Foothill students already take transit to school. This pass would benefit them greatly. However, if you do not take transit to school, this pass can benefit you as well! By lowering the cost of public transportation, there will be more incentive for other students to take the bus. If more students take the bus without a corresponding increase in students driving, traffic and parking issues on campus may be reduced. If you'd like to see if you can take transit to school, you can use the trip planner to plan your commute.

It's time to bring the Eco Pass to Foothill College. Vote Yes for the Eco Pass.

Benefits of the Eco Pass

The Eco Pass will reduce transportation costs for the many Foothill students who take VTA to school, and also for students who take VTA to other places. Additionally, it may help reduce traffic and parking issues on campus.

If more students choose to take the bus, and there is not a corresponding increase in driving, parking and traffic congestion on campus may be lessened. Parking has become a real problem on campus; the campus has been forced to hire parking attendants at some times to allow parking more cars in the limited parking spaces. Also, Foothill has teamed with Zimride in an effort to reduce vehicle trips to campus; providing public transportation as a cost-competitive alternative should be part of the same strategy.

Foothill is well-served by VTA transportation. VTA lines 40 and 52 stop at Foothill College. The 40 goes to downtown Los Altos, San Antonio Shopping Center, the Rengstorff area and the Shoreline area. The 52 goes to downtown Mountain View. These lines provide connections to Caltrain, other bus lines (including the El Camino Corridor and VTA's 522 Rapid service) and the VTA light rail.

Cost of the Eco Pass

At De Anza College, the Eco Pass program operates with a mandatory $5 fee for full-time students and $4 for part-time students. Current estimates for Foothill College are slightly higher. Compare that to the current $65 cost of a monthly adult VTA pass.
